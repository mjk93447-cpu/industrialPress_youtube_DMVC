# YouTube Data Acquisition And Rights Policy

## Purpose

This document fixes the PoC scope for acquiring hydraulic-press video data. The PoC separates automatic candidate discovery from training-data acquisition so the project can use YouTube metadata responsibly while only storing audiovisual content when the project has a valid right to do so.

## Acquisition Scope

The pipeline has two gates:

1. Candidate discovery through the YouTube Data API.
2. Approved clip ingestion only after a rights decision.

The system may automatically collect candidate metadata from public search results, but it must not automatically download, cache, import, or store YouTube audiovisual content unless one of the approved rights paths below applies.

## Approved Rights Paths

Training clips can enter the dataset only when their `rights_status` is one of these values:

- `creative_commons`: The video is discoverable with YouTube's Creative Commons license filter and the intended use is compatible with that license.
- `creator_permission`: The creator has explicitly granted permission for research/model-training use.
- `owned_capture`: The team produced the footage directly.
- `licensed_source`: A written license or contract permits storage, annotation, model training, and internal evaluation.
- `synthetic`: The clip was generated by simulation or controlled rendering and is not sourced from YouTube audiovisual content.

Videos with `standard_youtube_license` are allowed in the candidate review queue only. They are not training assets unless their rights status is later upgraded through creator permission or a separate license.

## Candidate Search Policy

The initial search seed set is:

- `hydraulic press crush`
- `hydraulic press experiment`
- `hydraulic press crushing objects`
- `hydraulic press glass`
- `hydraulic press metal`
- `hydraulic press rubber`
- `hydraulic press ceramic`
- `hydraulic press plastic`
- `hydraulic press wood`
- `hydraulic press fruit`
- `crushing objects hydraulic press slow motion`
- `industrial press failure test`

The first PoC target is 200-500 candidate videos and 50-150 approved clips. Search results must be deduplicated by `video_id` and reviewed for relevance before rights outreach.

## Metadata Fields

Candidate metadata may include:

- `video_id`
- `url`
- `title`
- `description`
- `channel_id`
- `channel_title`
- `published_at`
- `duration`
- `definition`
- `caption`
- `tags`
- `category_id`
- `license_filter_used`
- `embeddable`
- `region_restriction`
- `statistics_snapshot`
- `api_fetched_at`
- `metadata_refresh_due_at`
- `rights_status`
- `rights_evidence_uri`
- `review_status`

API-derived metadata must be refreshed or deleted according to the active YouTube API policies and the project's retention rules. The PoC should avoid deriving YouTube platform metrics beyond what is needed for candidate triage.

## Review And Exclusion Criteria

Exclude a candidate if:

- The object being pressed is not visible before failure.
- The clip is mostly a compilation, reaction, or edited montage.
- The failure frame is hidden by cuts, overlays, or severe motion blur.
- Background music or narration dominates the audio and no useful impact/deformation sound remains.
- The video uses heavy slow motion without enough timing context to reconstruct the event.
- The license or permission status is unclear after review.
- The content involves unsafe, illegal, or ethically inappropriate material.

## Rights Workflow

1. Ingest candidate metadata from YouTube Data API.
2. Assign `review_status = needs_relevance_review`.
3. Human reviewer marks relevance and candidate quality.
4. For promising standard-license videos, send creator outreach with a fixed permission template.
5. Store permission evidence outside the raw dataset and reference it with `rights_evidence_uri`.
6. Promote clips to `approved_for_training` only after rights verification.
7. Keep a deletion path for clips if permission is revoked or a licensing issue is found.

## PoC Decision

For this PoC, automatic collection means automatic candidate and metadata collection. Training-data ingestion is semi-automatic and gated by rights verification. This is the project baseline for all downstream dataset, model, and evaluation work.
